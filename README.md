                                          # Saas-Customer-Churn-Analysis
                                                                                           
                                                Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges forany  SaaS businesses because losing customers directly impacts recurring revenue and long-term growth.

In this project, I used PostgreSQL and Power BI to analyze customer behavior, identify the factors driving churn, and recommend practical actions the business could take to improve customer retention.

Rather than simply building charts, this project focuses on answering business questions and turning data into actionable insights.

The company wanted to understand:

                                                                                     Key Findings
* Starter customers experience the highest churn:

Although Starter customers make up the largest customer segment, they also record the highest churn rate, making them the biggest retention opportunity.

* Low customer satisfaction is the strongest churn indicator:

 Customers with low satisfaction scores churn significantly more than satisfied customers, suggesting that customer experience is a major driver of retention.

* Frequent support issues increase churn risk:

 Customers who submit more support tickets are more likely to leave, indicating unresolved product or service issues.

* Low engagement signals future churn:

Customers who log in infrequently and rarely adopt key product features are much more likely to churn than highly engaged users.

& Customer Success should focus on high-value accounts:

Medium-sized businesses generated the highest lifetime value, while large Enterprise customers showed high churn, making both groups important priorities for retention efforts.
